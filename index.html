About

I am a candidate of Master of Science in Electrical and Computer Engineering (ECE) at Carnegie Mellon University. I am currently working at Cylab Biometrics Center with Professor Marios Savvides to research about artificial intelligence for large-scale retail service. I received my bachelor's in Computer Science from the University of Michigan, where I worked on crowdsourcing and misinformation identification with Professor Walter Lasecki. My research spans the intersection of human computer interaction, social computing, and AI.

Projects
Walmart Product Detection

• Collaborated with Bossa Nova Robotics to develop the detection algorithm that identifies all Walmart items on the shelf in real-time using various state-of-the-art deep learning techniques

• Implemented the web system for simulation of the real-time product detection that enables measuring the performance of detection algorithm and show users the detailed information of each identified items

Data Cleaner Web Application

• Developed the data cleaning web application that enables crowd workers to filter out low-quality Walmart product training images after comparison with the target images.

• This application expedites the data cleaning process with fewer crowd workers, and eventually highly contributed the improvement of the performance of the Walmart product detection.

Face Recognition Web Application

• Developed the web application that clusters images with human faces to detect specific people that we target to find.

• Developed the function that analyzes and returns the lists of people in the images that associated with a specific person that a user identifies

Human Resource Management Application

• Developed the web application that enables the efficient management of the lab environments

• This application collects daily progress reports from lab members, then efficiently visualizes each team members' progresses and obstacles to project leaders, helping them to set up next milestones and future direction of the projects.

Misinformation (Conspiracy Thinking) Mitigation

• Developed the misinformation identification pipeline, especially for detecting groundless conspiracy thinkings in the Internet article, using collective reasoning of non-expert crowd workers (crowdsourcing)

• Leveraged the concept of logical fallacies to exclude crowd workers' prejudice on controversial topics and to reduce the noise on collective data.
